# Code of Conduct

This project is a small public-candidate workspace for practical adaptation
tools, templates, and synthetic examples.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful and specific in issues, reviews, and pull requests.
- Keep examples synthetic, repo-relative, and public-safe.
- Keep claims modest and tied to what the local tools actually do.
- Respect the boundary: no private sources, transcripts, customer data, grant
  strategy, internal paths, secrets, account actions, or adoption/funding claims.
- Accept maintainer decisions about scope, safety wording, and repo hygiene.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, insults, or discriminatory language.
- Posting private records, credentials, customer data, internal paths, or
  proprietary strategy.
- Representing starter-kit outputs as certification, professional advice,
  institutional approval, or evidence of adoption.
- Pressuring contributors to publish, fundraise, contact customers, connect
  accounts, or take external actions.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, reject, hide, or remove contributions that violate this
conduct note or the repository safety policy. Repeated or serious violations may
lead to blocked participation.
