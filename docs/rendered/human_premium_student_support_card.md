# Human Premium Trust Surface Card: Student support first-response surface

**Status:** HUMAN PREMIUM PROTECTED — REVIEW BEFORE CHANGE  
**Organization/team:** School or learning program support team  
**Review owner:** Program director or student support lead  
**Last reviewed:** 2026-06-12  
**Decision:** pilot_with_review  

## Trust context
The team wants AI to reduce routine student-support load without turning a struggling student into a ticket, a risk score, or an automated discipline path.

## Stakeholders
- Student or learner
- Parent, guardian, or trusted adult when appropriate
- Teacher or coach
- Student support lead
- Program director

## Posture counts
- Remove machine-work: 1
- Support human work: 2
- Protect from automation pressure: 1
- Leave human: 1
- High-trust steps: 2

## Surface steps
### Step 1: Collect routine context about the support request
- Current owner: Student support coordinator
- Trust weight: low
- AI posture: remove
- Reason: AI can collect basic scheduling, course, assignment, and access details when the student has a visible path to human help.
- Risk if automated: A long or confusing intake form may make the student feel ignored before any adult has listened.
### Step 2: Prepare a private support summary for the teacher or coach
- Current owner: Coordinator with teacher review
- Trust weight: medium
- AI posture: support
- Reason: AI can organize dates, missing work, access issues, and prior attempts so the human can respond faster.
- Risk if automated: The summary may overstate certainty, flatten context, or miss signs that the student needs a human conversation.
### Step 3: Respond to discouragement, embarrassment, anxiety, conflict, or possible harm
- Current owner: Named trained human support lead
- Trust weight: high
- AI posture: leave_human
- Reason: The premium is presence, care, and accountability. Students should not receive automated reassurance or crisis-adjacent guidance.
- Risk if automated: A student may feel dismissed, receive inappropriate guidance, or be routed away from a responsible adult.
### Step 4: Decide whether to escalate to a parent, counselor, administrator, or safeguarding process
- Current owner: Program director or designated safeguarding lead
- Trust weight: high
- AI posture: protect
- Reason: AI may flag patterns for review, but escalation changes obligations and must belong to a named human authority.
- Risk if automated: Unaccountable escalation, missed duty of care, unfair labeling, or privacy breach.
### Step 5: Generate practice options or study next steps after human review
- Current owner: Teacher or coach
- Trust weight: medium
- AI posture: support
- Reason: AI can draft practice menus, reminders, and study plans once the human has checked the student's actual need and constraints.
- Risk if automated: Generic advice may blame the student, overload them, or ignore access/support barriers.

## Human premium dimensions
- care

## Approved AI roles
- Collect routine logistics with a human-contact fallback
- Draft private support summaries for teacher review
- Suggest practice options after a human checks fit
- Flag missing context for a named human owner instead of making escalation decisions

## Protected human work
- Emotional first response
- Crisis-adjacent or safeguarding judgment
- Discipline, grading, eligibility, or placement decisions
- Parent or guardian escalation
- Promises about outcomes, accommodations, timelines, or obligations

## Human checkpoints
- Every support summary is reviewed by a named teacher, coach, or support lead before use
- Students always see a direct human-help path before and after any form
- Any harm, safety, discrimination, disability, or family-status signal bypasses automation and goes to the designated human owner
- Pilot review after 20 support requests or 30 days, whichever comes first
