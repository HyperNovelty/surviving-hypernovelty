# Human Recourse Path Card: HR benefits assistant recourse path

**Status:** HUMAN RECOURSE REQUIRED — OWNER REVIEW BEFORE LIVE USE  
**Service context:** An HR benefits assistant explains open enrollment windows, plan documents, and routine routing. It must not decide eligibility, discipline, leave approval, accommodation, payroll correction, or protected employment matters.  
**Review owner:** HR operations lead with legal/compliance owner review where appropriate  
**Last reviewed:** 2026-06-25  
**Card state:** needs_owner_review  

## AI role
Point employees to approved benefits materials, summarize routine plan-navigation steps, and prepare a private checklist for the HR owner when the employee asks for review.

## The AI/tool does not decide
- Eligibility, benefit denial, leave/accommodation approval, payroll correction, employment status, discipline, retaliation claim, or legal-rights interpretation.
- Whether an employee should waive coverage, disclose medical details in an unsafe channel, or ignore a formal notice.

## Human-review triggers
### Trigger 1: The answer affects pay, benefits eligibility, protected leave, accommodation, employment status, or deadline loss.
- Human owner: HR benefits owner or authorized HR partner
- Why this leaves the AI path: The employee needs accountable review before acting on a consequential answer.
### Trigger 2: The employee reports a conflicting document, portal error, or incorrect personal record.
- Human owner: HR operations lead
- Why this leaves the AI path: Records and official plan documents must be checked by a human owner.
### Trigger 3: The issue includes health, disability, family status, harassment, retaliation, or other sensitive employment context.
- Human owner: Designated HR/legal/compliance channel
- Why this leaves the AI path: The assistant must avoid collecting or deciding sensitive protected-context matters.

## Visible recourse path
- Label shown to the person: Need HR to review this benefits answer?
- Contact route: Submit a benefits review ticket through the approved HR portal or contact the HR partner named in the benefits guide.
- Appeal/review step: An authorized HR owner checks the plan document/process, corrects records if appropriate, and identifies any formal review path.
- Fallback if no response: Escalate to the HR operations lead or the benefits vendor contact listed in the employee guide; use approved urgent leave/accommodation channels for time-sensitive issues.
- Response-time expectation: Synthetic example: two business days for routine review; faster owner-approved route for deadlines that affect coverage, pay, leave, or accommodation.

## Evidence to include
- AI answer text or screenshot with date/time.
- Relevant plan/document title and section if known.
- Portal error or notice that conflicts with the answer.
- Avoid unnecessary medical, family, or protected-status details unless using the approved confidential channel.

## Repair commitments
- No denial, penalty, or employment-impacting action should rely only on the assistant answer.
- Correct misleading benefits content after owner review.
- Route sensitive cases away from the generic assistant into approved confidential channels.

## Boundary notes
- Review aid only; not legal, employment, financial, tax, healthcare, or benefits-plan advice.
- Do not store real employee or health details in this repo example.
- Actual timelines and escalation authority require HR/legal/compliance approval.

## Boundary
This card does not create a legal appeals process, rights guarantee, medical/financial/employment/schooling decision, compliance assurance, or promise of outcome. It is a local review aid for making the human path visible. Domain owners approve real timelines, escalation language, and authority.
