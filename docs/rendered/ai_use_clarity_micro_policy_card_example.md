# AI Use Clarity Micro-Policy Card: First-year writing assignment AI-use rule

**Status:** REVIEW REQUIRED  
**Owner:** Course instructor  
**Purpose:** Make one assignment-level AI-use boundary clear before learners start work.  
**Review cadence:** Before each assignment cycle or when institution policy changes.

## Task context
**Setting:** First-year writing course  
**Task:** Source-based research paragraph and revision  
**Learner-owned work:** Thesis, Evidence selection, Source interpretation, Final wording

## Allowed uses
- Ask AI to generate possible research questions before choosing one (before_drafting; disclosure: yes)
- Ask for grammar or clarity feedback after writing a draft (after_drafting; disclosure: yes)
- Ask for a checklist of source-citation items to verify manually (manual_verification; disclosure: yes)

## Disallowed uses
- Submit AI-generated paragraphs as original work - Final wording and reasoning must remain learner-owned
- Invent or accept citations without checking the source - Citation trails must be manually verifiable
- Use AI to avoid reading assigned sources or explaining reasoning in your own words - The assignment assesses source interpretation and explanation

## Required evidence
- One short disclosure note describing any AI help used (when: any_ai_use)
- Source notes showing which claims came from which assigned source (when: source_based_claim)
- A visible revision note explaining what changed after feedback (when: revision_after_feedback)

## Human review triggers
- Student cannot explain a claim in their own words -> clarify
- Citation trail is missing or source cannot be found -> repair
- AI use falls outside the allowed list -> escalate_to_local_policy_owner

## Review flags
- Requires local policy-owner/instructor approval before classroom use
- Not legal/compliance advice

## Boundary note
Review aid only. Local school, instructor, or workplace policy controls final allowed use. Do not use this card as legal, academic-integrity, or compliance advice.

## Notes
Synthetic example designed to avoid shaming informal AI use while making boundaries concrete.
