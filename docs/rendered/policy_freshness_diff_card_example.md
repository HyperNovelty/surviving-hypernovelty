# Policy Freshness Diff Card: Fictional college classroom AI-use note, June update

**Status:** OWNER REVIEW REQUIRED  
**Policy owner:** Teaching and learning committee  
**Evidence strength:** moderate  
**Freshness verdict:** interim_update_needed

## Context
A small college has a one-paragraph syllabus rule that says students may use AI only with instructor permission, but instructors are receiving repeated questions about tutoring, brainstorming, grammar help, and citation checking.

## Current rule or assumption
Students may use AI tools only when an instructor gives explicit permission for the assignment.

## Change signal
Students report learning AI practices from peers and short videos before formal guidance reaches them; instructors want a reusable way to distinguish allowed help, disclosure, and evidence-of-learning expectations.

## Possibly stale assumption
A single permission sentence is enough for learners to know which AI uses are acceptable and what evidence they must preserve.

## Affected people
- students who need clear day-to-day permission boundaries
- instructors who must explain acceptable help without rewriting every syllabus
- academic support staff who coach students before assignments are due

## Proposed diff
**Change type:** clarify  
**Interim language:** For each major assignment, the instructor should state: allowed AI uses, disallowed AI uses, required disclosure wording, required evidence of thinking or source trail, and when to ask before using a tool.  
**Do not change yet:** Do not replace course-level academic-integrity policy or create detector-based discipline rules from this interim note alone. Keep final authority with the instructor and existing review process.

## Review path
