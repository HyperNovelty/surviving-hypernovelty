# Assessment Goal Drift Repair Card: First-year source essay repair card

**Review owner:** Instructor / instructional designer
**Review date:** 2026-07-06

## Context
A first-year writing course sees polished essays that no longer show whether students can evaluate sources or revise claims.

## Original assignment
Submit a 1,200-word argumentative essay using three outside sources.

## Original learning goal
Students should practice claim framing, source evaluation, revision, and explaining why evidence supports a position.

## What AI can now offload
- AI can produce a fluent essay without showing source judgment
- AI can hide whether the student revised a weak claim
- Detector-first enforcement may produce false certainty

## Evidence still needed
- Claim map with source-to-claim links
- Two source notes showing reliability/context checks
- One revision reflection explaining what changed
- Short oral or in-class process check

## Redesigned proof artifact
- Claim map submitted before final essay
- Annotated source notes
- Final essay
- Reflection naming one rejected AI suggestion or one human revision choice

## Allowed AI role
AI may help brainstorm counterarguments and grammar questions, but it may not invent sources, replace source notes, or write the reflection.

## Equity/access check
- Free/non-AI path available
- No premium AI tool required
- Students may complete process evidence with class notes and library sources

## Recourse path if questioned
- If flagged, student can show notes, claim map, draft history, and explain one source choice
- Instructor reviews process evidence before penalty

## Do not claim
- This does not prove authorship
- This does not make the assignment AI-proof
- This is not legal or academic-integrity advice

## Boundary note
Local teaching-design aid only. Department policy, accessibility review, and student recourse must remain human-owned.
