# Human Recourse Path Card: Scam-contact human recourse path

**Status:** HUMAN RECOURSE REQUIRED — OWNER REVIEW BEFORE LIVE USE  
**Service context:** A household, school office, or small organization receives suspicious calls/texts/emails that claim to represent banks, delivery services, managers, agencies, platforms, or family contacts.  
**Review owner:** Local account owner, office manager, family lead, or designated support person  
**Last reviewed:** 2026-06-25  
**Card state:** ready_for_pilot  

## AI role
An AI-supported helper may draft a calm summary of the suspicious contact, list what evidence to preserve, and suggest known-good verification routes. It does not decide whether the message is safe, send replies, call numbers, click links, pay money, report people, or access accounts.

## Verification context
- Claimed authority: Bank, carrier, manager, agency, platform, or family contact named in the suspicious message
- Source of truth: Known-good route independent of the suspicious message: official app, bookmarked site, card-back number, internal directory, in-person check, or established family contact method
- Evidence to preserve before action:
- Screenshot or copy of the message
- Sender address/phone number
- Timestamp
- Requested action
- Any amount, account, shipment, or deadline claimed

## The AI/tool does not decide
- Whether the contact is definitely safe or fraudulent
- Whether to pay money, share codes, enter login details, or provide identity information
- Whether to contact law enforcement, a bank, a school, an employer, or a platform
- Whether to delete evidence before the relevant owner reviews it

## Human-review triggers
### Trigger 1: The message asks for money, gift cards, crypto, wire transfer, login, one-time code, identity data, or software installation.
- Human owner: Account owner or designated trusted reviewer
- Why this leaves the AI path: These requests can create immediate financial, identity, account, or device risk.
### Trigger 2: The message claims authority: bank, government, employer, school, delivery company, platform, healthcare provider, or family emergency.
- Human owner: Person responsible for that domain or relationship
- Why this leaves the AI path: The claimed authority must be verified through an independent source of truth.
### Trigger 3: The message uses urgency, secrecy, threat, shame, or pressure to avoid normal verification.
- Human owner: Local reviewer or manager before any response
- Why this leaves the AI path: Pressure is part of the risk surface; slowing down is the safety action.

## Visible recourse path
- Label shown to the person: Pause and verify with a human through a known route
- Contact route: Use the known-good contact list, official app/site, card-back phone number, internal directory, or in-person confirmation — not the suspicious message thread.
- Appeal/review step: Preserve evidence, write the requested action in one sentence, and ask the local reviewer whether any action is needed.
- Fallback if no response: Take no irreversible action. Do not click, pay, share codes, install software, or delete evidence until a human owner verifies the route.
- Response-time expectation: For money, codes, login, identity, device access, or threats: verify before acting. For low-risk messages: review when convenient, but still avoid links and replies from the suspicious thread.

## Evidence to include
- Message screenshot or copied text with private data redacted if sharing internally
- Sender address/number/profile
- Requested action and deadline
- Known-good source checked and result
- Any money, code, account, identity, software, or secrecy request

## Repair commitments
- If someone clicked, paid, shared a code, or entered credentials, escalate through official account/security/recovery routes.
- If the message was real, update the household/team source-of-truth list so the next person can verify faster.
- If the message was fake, document the pattern without preserving unnecessary private information.

## Boundary notes
- This is a local recourse card, not fraud investigation, cybersecurity assurance, legal advice, banking advice, or identity-theft recovery guidance.
- The card does not determine whether a message is safe or fraudulent.
- Emergency threats, actual losses, account compromise, and identity exposure require official reporting or recovery routes.

## Boundary
This card does not create a legal appeals process, rights guarantee, medical/financial/employment/schooling decision, compliance assurance, or promise of outcome. It is a local review aid for making the human path visible. Domain owners approve real timelines, escalation language, and authority.
