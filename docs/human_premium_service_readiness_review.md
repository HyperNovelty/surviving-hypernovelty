Use case: consolidate the Human Premium Trust Surface examples into a practical readiness screen for service teams.

## What this page is for

This is a short operator-facing readiness page for teams that want AI to reduce administrative drag without weakening human trust. It turns the existing healthcare/front-desk and student-support examples into a reusable review sequence.

This is not a policy engine, clinical guide, counseling protocol, education-compliance checklist, legal opinion, or staffing recommendation.

## Core rule

AI can remove machine-work. Humans must own trust-bearing work.

A service workflow is not ready for AI support until the team can clearly name:

1. what AI may remove;
2. what AI may prepare for a human;
3. what AI may only flag;
4. what must stay human;
5. who reviews the pilot;
6. when the pilot stops.

## Consolidated examples

### Healthcare / front desk

Safe direction: reduce routine appointment, reminder, and duplicate-intake work.

Protect:

- anxious, embarrassed, angry, confused, grieving, or vulnerable conversations;
- clinical, privacy, safety, and special-handling decisions;
- any patient-facing promise about care, cost, timing, diagnosis, or outcome;
- escalation decisions.

Readiness signal: every automated reminder or intake path shows a visible human fallback, and all escalation flags go to a named human before patient-facing action.

### Student support / first response

Safe direction: collect routine context, prepare private summaries, and suggest practice options after teacher/coach review.

Protect:

- emotional first response;
- crisis-adjacent, safeguarding, disability, family-status, fairness, discipline, grading, eligibility, or placement decisions;
- parent/guardian escalation;
- promises about accommodations, outcomes, obligations, or timelines.

Readiness signal: every support summary is reviewed by a named teacher, coach, or support lead before use, and students always see a direct human-help path.

## Service readiness screen

Use this before building a live workflow.

| Question | Ready answer |
| --- | --- |
| What exact machine-work are we removing? | A narrow, repetitive task with an approved template or human review path. |
| What human work are we protecting? | Presence, care, privacy judgment, escalation, relationship repair, fairness, or accountability. |
| Who is the named human owner? | A role/person with authority to stop, revise, or reject the workflow. |
| What data is excluded from the pilot? | Real patient/student/client records unless domain approval and privacy handling are complete. |
| What can AI send directly? | Nothing by default; only pre-approved routine text after owner review. |
| What can AI flag? | Missing routine context or possible escalation; it cannot decide the escalation. |
| What is the pilot size? | A small bounded count or time window, e.g. 10 patient interactions / 20 support requests / 30 days. |
| What stops the pilot? | Confusion, distress, unfair routing, privacy concern, unreviewed escalation, or any human-owner objection. |

## Practical service package idea

Working name: **Human Premium Service Readiness Screen**

Audience: clinics, learning programs, support teams, small service organizations, and operators who want AI assistance without turning trust into a ticket system.

Small offer shape, review-only:

- one-page trust-surface map;

- protected-human-work list;
- pilot stop conditions;
- owner-review checklist;

Do not market this as compliance automation, clinical triage, counseling support, special-education guidance, legal guidance, HR decision support, or guaranteed cost reduction.
