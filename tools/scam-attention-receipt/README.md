# Scam Attention Receipt

A local-first receipt for suspicious calls, texts, emails, DMs, ads, or social messages.

The goal is not to investigate live fraud. The goal is to reduce the daily attention tax by making the verification route visible before anyone clicks, calls back, pays, shares codes, or responds under pressure.

## Use when

- A message claims to be from a bank, carrier, delivery company, employer, school, government office, platform, or family contact.
- The message asks for money, login, codes, downloads, identity details, urgent action, or a reply.
- A person or team needs a calm checklist for what to verify through a known-good route.

## Local command

```bash
python3 tools/scam-attention-receipt/render_receipt.py examples/media/scam_attention_receipt_example.json
```

## Boundary

This is not legal advice, cybersecurity assurance, fraud investigation, identity-theft recovery, banking advice, or a guarantee that a message is safe or unsafe. Use official channels and domain-owner procedures for real incidents.
