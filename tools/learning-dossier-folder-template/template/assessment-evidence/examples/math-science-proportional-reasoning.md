# Example: Math / Science Assessment Evidence

## Learning mission

Show that the learner understands proportional reasoning rather than only copying a formula.

## Task / prompt

A recipe uses 3 cups of flour for 12 cookies. How much flour is needed for 30 cookies?

## Learner attempt

30 cookies is 2.5 times 12 cookies. 3 cups × 2.5 = 7.5 cups.

## Explanation in learner's own words

I compared the new number of cookies to the original number. Since 30 is two and a half groups of 12, I need two and a half groups of the flour too.

## Transfer problem

A paint mix uses 2 blue parts and 5 white parts for one batch. How many blue parts are needed if white parts increase to 20?

## Misconceptions or weak spots

- Watch for adding 18 instead of multiplying by a ratio.
- Check whether the learner can explain why the scale factor applies to both quantities.

## Feedback received

- AI feedback: The ratio method is correct; ask learner to solve a reversed-ratio problem.
- Teacher/mentor feedback: Needs one more transfer problem before marking strong.

## Evidence rating

- Strong evidence: Correct answer plus transferable ratio explanation.
- Partial evidence: Correct calculation without explanation.
- Weak or missing evidence: Final answer only, no method or transfer.
