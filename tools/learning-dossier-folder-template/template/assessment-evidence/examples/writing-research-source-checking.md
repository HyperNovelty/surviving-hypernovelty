# Example: Writing / Research Assessment Evidence

## Learning mission

Show that the learner can make a sourced claim, distinguish evidence strength, and revise a paragraph after feedback.

## Task / prompt

Write one paragraph explaining whether AI-generated answers make source checking more important.

## Learner attempt

AI-generated answers make source checking more important because they can sound confident even when they are wrong. A reader needs to know where the claim came from and whether the source actually supports it.

## Explanation in learner's own words

I am saying the problem is not just wrong answers. The problem is that the answer can look finished before the evidence is checked.

## Transfer problem

Revise the paragraph using one primary source and one secondary source. Mark which claim each source supports.

## Misconceptions or weak spots

- Learner may treat all links as equal evidence.
- Learner may cite a source without showing which claim it supports.

## Feedback received

- AI feedback: Add a concrete example of weak sourcing.
- Teacher/mentor feedback: Distinguish primary vs secondary sources before expanding.

## Evidence rating

- Strong evidence: Claim, source mapping, confidence notes, revision after feedback.
- Partial evidence: Coherent paragraph without source mapping.
- Weak or missing evidence: Polished paragraph only.
