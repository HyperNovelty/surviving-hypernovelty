## Candidate review cards

### 1) Agent Toolchain Exposure Map
- Lane: repo/free now; paid later for team implementation support.
- Adaptation burden revealed: teams are adding agents/tools faster than they can see what each can read, write, trigger, expose, or impersonate.
- Who carries it: security/IT, ops leads, managers approving automations, and nontechnical workers using shadow AI.
- Missing layer: a simple tool-call/workflow map that links agent identity, permissions, data touched, output destinations, and human checkpoints.
- Smallest proof artifact: one Markdown worksheet + JSON example extending Agent Permission Receipt into a multi-agent/toolchain table.
- Genericity-test risk: medium; a prompt can list risks, but a repo artifact can win by providing a repeatable permission schema, examples, and validator.
- Safety/claim caveats: do not claim security certification; position as visibility and review aid, not compliance assurance.
- Next build step: create `/tools/agent-toolchain-exposure-map/README.md`, schema, and sample team packet.

### 2) Shadow AI Disclosure Ladder
- Lane: HN article + repo/free primitive; paid later only for facilitation/custom policy rollout.
- Adaptation burden revealed: unapproved AI use is rational when official workflows lag, but it creates hidden data and accountability risk.
- Who carries it: workers trying to keep up, managers who are judged on output, IT/security teams that discover usage late.
- Missing layer: a nonpunitive disclosure ladder: safe-to-share use cases, red-line data, review triggers, and escalation paths.
- Smallest proof artifact: printable one-page ladder with examples: personal drafting, internal summarization, customer data, credentials, publishing, spending.
- Genericity-test risk: low-medium; generic policies exist, but a psychologically safe disclosure ladder is a sharper workflow primitive.
- Safety/claim caveats: avoid legal/HR promises; advise local review before adoption.
- Next build step: draft ladder template plus three sample versions: solo creator, small business, nonprofit/school.

## Paid/free boundary reminder

Keep free primitives as local worksheets, schemas, examples, and validators. Paid versions are justified only when the buyer needs customization, facilitation, hosting, institutional review, integration, or implementation support. Avoid paid claims based on certainty, compliance, ranking/SEO outcomes, detector accuracy, or security certification unless externally verified.
